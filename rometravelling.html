GREAT ROME

Colosseum

The Colosseum is one of the most iconic and recognizable landmarks in Rome. This ancient amphitheater was built almost 2,000 years ago, and was used for gladiatorial contests, animal hunts, and other public spectacles. Today, visitors can explore the ruins of the Colosseum and learn about the fascinating history of this impressive structure.

Vatican City

Vatican City is a must-see destination for art and history enthusiasts. This small city-state is home to some of the world's most famous museums, including the Vatican Museums and the Sistine Chapel, which houses the breathtaking frescoes painted by Michelangelo. Visitors can also explore St. Peter's Basilica, one of the largest churches in the world, and see works by artists such as Bernini and Raphael.

Roman Forum

The Roman Forum was the heart of ancient Rome and served as the center of political and social activity for centuries. Today, visitors can explore the ruins of the Forum and see ancient structures such as the Temple of Saturn and the Arch of Titus. The Forum is a great place to learn about the daily life of ancient Romans and their impressive architectural achievements.

Trevi Fountaint

The Trevi Fountain is a beautiful Baroque masterpiece located in the heart of Rome. Visitors can toss a coin into the fountain and make a wish, according to local legend. The fountain is also known for its intricate sculptures and stunning water features, which make it a popular spot for tourists and locals alike.
